Welcome to my hobby of Listening to music

Music is something that everyone can enjoy. There's a genre for everyone. Listening to music is a way to relax and escape. Here, I share my love for listening to music.

Remember to keep an open mind when listening to new music, don't be confined to one genre or a language.

About me

Of course, I've always listened to music, mostly mainstream pop and the things that were playing on the radio. It wasn't until middle school that I started listening to things that weren't considered "mainstream." I started listening to BTS, a K-pop group, in 2016, and that's when I started exploring other genres. I started listening to other K-pop groups, and then I started listening to other genres like rock, rap, and indie. High school came around, and I started listening to more indie and alternative music. I started listening to artists like Clairo, Wallows, Artic-Monkeys, and Slipknot. If you look at my liked songs on Spotify, an account that I have since 2017, you'll see a variety of genres. I'm always looking for new music to listen to, and I'm always open to new genres.

Best times to listen to Music

Music makes daily routines more enjoyable. I recommend listening to music whenever you're moving: walking to class, riding the bus, or the light rail. Music can make the most boring things fun. I also recommend listening to music when you're feeling down, putting an upbeat song can change your mood. Also, listening helps you focus on your tasks.

When studying I like to listen to the Oppenheimer soundtrack. It makes me feel like I'm a cool scientist and not a broke collage student.

How to make the most out of music

If you want to expand your horizon of music, be open to listening to different genres or experimental sounds. The best way I find new music is by looking up playlists on Spotify or whatever music streaming service you use. If I like a song, I'll look into their discography and see if I like any other songs. Also seeing what that artist recommends is a great way to discover new music.

Why listening to Music Matters

Music reduces stress, boosts mood, and allows us to connect to ourselves and others. I've made friends because we have similar music tastes. There are so many songs out there that can make you feel a certain way. The lyrics can be relatable, and the beat can make you dance. Music is a universal language that everyone can understand and enjoy.
